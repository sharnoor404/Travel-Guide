Amazing Australia!

The world is yours to explore!

The world's smallest continent and largest island, Australia is the land of staggering contrasts and spectacular beauty.It is truly the land of dreams.From the sacred legends of the aboriginal Dreamtime, when the great spirits conjured the coral reefs, rainforests, and scorched red deserts, to armchair travelers who describe Australia as their dream destination, the Land Down Under deserves all the hype. Top it all off with a laid-back feel and friendly people, and some of the quirkiest wildlife on the planet.

Sydney Opera House

The most famous spot of Australia is the Sydney Opera House.This famous building on Sydney's Bennelong Point graces the list of UNESCO World Heritage Sites and is one of the world's great architectural icons.

Location: Bennelong Point, Sydney

Opened on: 20 October, 1973

Best time to visit:October, November, late February and March.

Other places to visit nearby: Sydney Harbour Bridge,Government House, Walk to the Opera House to the Botanic Garden and many more.

Uluru-Kata Tjuta National Park

One of the most photographed natural wonders in the country is the Uluru-Kata Tjuta National Park,located in the heart of Australia's Red Centre.The striking red monolith forms the centerpiece of Uluru-Kata Tjuta National Park.

Location: Uluru NT, Australia

Best time to visit: May to September

Other places to visit nearby: Kata Tjuta, Kings Canyon, Alice Springs,Tennant Creek and many more.

Bondi Beach

Home to one of the oldest surf life saving clubs in the world is the Bondi Beach.From bronzed bodies, blond sand, backpackers to surf, you get it all here.It is a great spot for seaside stroll or picnic.

Location: Sydney, New South Wales, Australia

Best time to visit: December to February

What to explore in Bondi?: Bondi to Coogee Walk, Tamarama Beach, Bondi Iceberg Club, North Bondi Lookout and many more.

Twelve Apostles - Visitor Facility

Collection of limestone stacks off the shore of Port Campbell National Park, by the Great Ocean Road in Victoria, Australia. Their proximity to one another has made the site a popular tourist attraction.Seven of the original eight stacks remain standing at the Twelve Apostles viewpoint.

Location: Port Campbell National Park, Victoria, Australia

Best time to visit: sunrise or sunset

Other islands to visit nearby: Port Campbell National Park, Twelve Apostles Marine National Park and many more.

Sydney Harbour Bridge

One of the most famous toruist attractions,the Sydeny Harbour Bridge(affectionately called "the Coathanger") is the largest steel arch bridge in the world.From the top of the bridge, one can surely enjoy the spectacular view over the harbor and city.

Location: Sydney, Australia

Opened: 19 March 1932

Length: 1,149 m

Other places to visit nearby : Pylon Lookout, BridgeClimb Sydney, Sydney Opera House and many more.

Whitsunday Island

The Whitsunday Islands are a collection of 74 continental islands of various sizes off the central coast of Queensland, Australia, approximately 900 kilometres north of Brisbane. In 2009 as part of the Q150 celebrations, the Whitsunday Islands was announced as one of the Q150 Icons of Queensland for its role as a "Natural attraction"

Location: Queensland,Australia

Best time to visit: September(early spring)

Other places to visit nearby: Airlie Beach, Hamilton Island, Whitehaven Beach, Heart Reef and many more.
