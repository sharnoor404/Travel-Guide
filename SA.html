Adventure awaits!

A continent of superlatives: the highest, the driest, the largest, the deepest, the rarest.The number of countries in South America with such vastly different cultures and terrain, may leave you overwhelmed.So, when planning your trip, consider one of these highly recommended destinations that are perfect for both first times and repeat visitors.

Machu Picchu

Renowned for its sophisticated dry-stone walls that fuse huge blocks without the use of mortar, intriguing buildings that play on astronomical alignments and panoramic views, Machu Pichu is an Incan citadel set high in the Andes Mountains.

Location: Peru

Elevation: 2,430 m

Abandoned: 1572

Best time to visit: Late March, April, May and September, October, and early November.

Other places to visit nearby: Huayna Pichu, Inti Punku, Wiñay Wayna, Temple of the Moon and many more.

Christ The Redeemer

Listed as one of the 7 wonders, Christ the redeemer is an Art Deco statue of Jesus Christ in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil.It is located at the peak of the 700-metre (2,300 ft) Corcovado mountain in the Tijuca Forest National Park overlooking the city of Rio de Janeiro.

Location: Rio de Janeiro, Brazil

Height: 38 m

Construction Started: 4 April 1922

Best time to visit: Before 8:30 am or after 3:30 pm

Other places to visit nearby: Sugarloaf, Copacabana, Tijuca National Park and many more.

Atacama Desert

This desert plateau is one of the driest places in the world, as well as the only true desert to receive less precipitation than the polar deserts.

Location: Chile

Area: 105,000 km²

Best time to visit: September to November and March to May

Other places to visit nearby: Valle de la Luna, R. P. Gustavo Le Paige Archaeological Museum, Pukará de Quitor Pukará and many more.

Quilotoa Lake

Quilotoa is a water-filled caldera and the most western volcano in the Ecuadorian Andes. The 3-kilometre (2 mi)-wide caldera was formed by the collapse of this dacite volcano following a catastrophic VEI-6 eruption about 600 years ago

Location: Pujilí Canton, Cotopaxi Province, Ecuador

Elevation: 3,914 m

Last Eruption: 1280

Best time to visit: June–September

Other places to visit nearby: Toachi River Canyon, Reserva Ecologica Los Illinizas and many more.

Iguazu Falls

Iguazu Falls are waterfalls of the Iguazu River.Together, they make up the largest waterfall in the world.

Location: Argentina

Width: 2,700 m

Other places to visit nearby: Lookout Devil's Throat, Parque Nacional Iguazú, Salto San Martín and many more.

Torres Del Paine National Park

Torres del Paine National Park, known for its soaring mountains, bright blue icebergs that cleave from glaciers and golden pampas (grasslands) that shelter rare wildlife such as llama-like guanacos. Some of its most iconic sites are the 3 granite towers from which the park takes its name and the horn-shaped peaks called Cuernos del Paine.

Location: Magallanes and Chilean Antarctica, Chile

Best time to visit: November to early March

Other places to visit nearby: Grey Glacier, Grey Lake, Lake Pehoé many more.
